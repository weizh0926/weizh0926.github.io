Alpha decays when **liquidity, volatility, and microstructure** change. Models that overfit historical correlations fail quietly until they do not. Practical systems combine **strict out-of-sample protocols**, **latency-aware features**, and **position limits** that do not assume the backtest distribution is the live one. The edge is often in execution and risk, not in a fancier architecture alone.
